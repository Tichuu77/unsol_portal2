Sickle Cell Mukt Bharat

Nagpur District Project

This project is under the guidance of our Honorable Guardian Minister and District Collector, in collaboration with Government Medical College Nagpur.

Objectives
  • Raise awareness about Sickle Cell Disease among the populace of Nagpur District as per the guideline of NHM.
  • Provide accessible screening facilities to identify individuals at risk.
  • Educate people at large and focusing specially on students about the genetic, medical, and societal implications of Sickle Cell Disease.
  • Foster a culture of proactive healthcare seeking and disease prevention within the community.

Screening Limitations
  • Awareness Gap
  • Lack of awareness among the general population about the importance of screening of SCD.
  • Limited understanding of the symptoms and consequences of the SCD.
  • Education Deficiency
  • Inadequate education regarding the significance of early detection and preventive measures.
  • Lack of knowledge about the importance of regular health check-ups.
  • Complexity of Screening
  • Screening tests may not be easily accessible to everyone, especially in areas with limited healthcare facilities.
  • Some screening tests might be difficult methods to implement large scale screening.
  • The financial cost of screening can be high, making it less accessible to people with limited resources.
  • Challenges in SDC Screening
  • Limited availability of screening facilities or expertise in certain regions.
  • Cultural or social factors may impact willingness to undergo screening for SCD.
  • Challenge is to make people understand the importance of SCD screening.
  • There are misconceptions and myths among the people regarding screening for SDC.
Opportunities in sickle cell disease(SCD)
  • Research and Innovation
  • Research into the pathophysiology, genetics, and treatment of SCD.
  • Innovation in diagnostics, such as point-of-care testing (POCT).
  • Genetic screening, and predictive measure to improve early detection.
  • Clinical Care and Management
  • Improved pain management strategies, prophylactic treatments.
  • Supportive care measures to enhance for individuals live with SCD.
  • Opportunities for multidisciplinary care models, psychosocial, and genetic counseling services.
  • Public Health Initiatives
  • Opportunities for public health interventions aimed at raising awareness.
  • Encouraging early detection and putting preventive measures into healthcare systems.
  • Collaboration with community organizations for policy to benefit for individuals with SCD.
  • Education and Training
  • Opportunities for education and training programs targeting healthcare professionals and community members to increase knowledge about SCD.
